About Me

I'm an AI researcher based in Germany with a passion for solving complex problems. My work focuses on designing and training deep learning architectures capable of tackling challenging tasks across vision, language and structured data domains. Deep learning models are multilayered networks that can simulate complex decision making; by leveraging large datasets and thoughtful network design I create systems that learn to interpret data at multiple levels of abstraction. When I'm not coding or experimenting with new models, you'll find me mentoring students and exploring the latest research.

Over the past several years I've authored numerous publications and open‑sourced projects. I'm always keen to collaborate, whether it's building custom neural networks, researching novel architectures or deploying models into production. Reach out if you have an interesting problem that machine learning might solve.

Research

My research journey spans a diverse set of topics within artificial intelligence and machine learning. I am particularly interested in the design of novel neural architectures that can generalise across tasks and data modalities. At the core of this work is the idea that deep learning involves stacking many layers of neurons to model increasingly abstract representations.

Some of my recent publications investigate ways of improving stability and interpretability in deep networks. Other papers explore self‑supervised learning, where models learn from unlabeled data by predicting missing information. You can find a list of my papers on Google Scholar.
